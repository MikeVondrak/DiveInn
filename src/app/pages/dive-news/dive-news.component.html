In Memory of Our Friend Andy Post

1975 - 2008

On June 18, 2008, we lost a very special friend of ours. His name was Andy Post.

Andy was a recent graduate of the Principal Licensure Program at the University of Denver and worked as an Assistant Principal at Cheltenham Elementary School. An avid fly fisherman, extreme skier, and mountain biker, Andy loved everything nature had to offer in Colorado. Above all, he was passionate about the environment, and loved educating children about science.

He planned to launch a “Green” school in Denver with the goal of fostering lifelong learners who would work together for a sustainable future. Andy was following in the footsteps of his parents and grandparents, who taught in Denver Public Schools for over 100 years.

A dedicated group of educators carried on Andy’s dream and the mission to have “hands-on, brains-on” learning environment help children connect sustainability to their daily lives. Andy was part of this original group of founding partners, but when he died the remaining seven partners continued the work and in 2010 Andy’s dream of a “Green” school became a reality.

The Denver Green School (DGS) opened its doors in southeast Denver as the first school to be focused on sustainability in the whole district. Since then, the school has done some amazing things to create the future Andy dreamed of for many years. The students have tackled many important sustainability projects and the school has been recognized by the White House as one of only three schools in the state and 78 schools in the country to receive a Green Ribbon Schools award.
